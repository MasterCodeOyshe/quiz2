**1. "把" (bǎ) Sentences for Handling Objects**

- **Structure description:** To ask about what happened to an object or where it was placed, use a question word like "nǎr" (where) after the verb, or use a yes/no format like "shì bu shì" (is it or is it not) before the "bǎ" structure.
- **Example:** _Nǐ bǎ shǒujī fàng zài nǎr le?_ (Where did you put your cellphone?).

**2. "会" (huì) Sentences for Future Possibility**

- **Structure description:** Turn these into questions by adding "ma" at the very end for a simple yes/no question, or by using a question word like "nǎge" (which) at the beginning to ask about a specific subject.
- **Example:** _Míngtiān huì xià yǔ ma?_ (Will it rain tomorrow?).

**3. "了" (le) Sentences for Completed Actions**

- **Structure description:** To ask about an action that was completed, replace the object or the quantity of the object with a question word like "shénme" (what) or "jǐ" (how many).
- **Example:** _Nǐ jièle jǐ běn zázhì?_ (How many magazines did you borrow?).

**4. Double-Verb (Pivotal) Sentences**

- **Structure description:** To ask about the second action in this sequence, replace the second verb and its object with "zuò shénme" (do what).
- **Example:** _Tā xiǎng qǐng nǚpéngyou zuò shénme?_ (What does he want to invite his girlfriend to do?).

**5. Sentences Showing Action Frequency**

- **Structure description:** Instead of stating the exact number of times an action happened, use the question word "jǐ" (how many) right before the action measure word (like "cì" for times).
- **Example:** _Zhège yuè nǐ chídàole jǐ cì?_ (How many times were you late this month?).

**6. Sentences Showing Action Duration**

- **Structure description:** To ask how long an action has been going on, insert a time-related question phrase like "duō cháng shíjiān" (how much time) directly after the verb.
- **Example:** _Nǐ xuéle duō cháng shíjiān Hànyǔ le?_ (How long have you been learning Chinese?).

**7. "比" (bǐ) Sentences for Comparisons**

- **Structure description:** Rather than using the word "bǐ" to form a question, these materials teach you to ask for a comparison by presenting two options separated by "háishi" (or), or by using "nǎge" (which one).
- **Example:** _Gēge gāo háishi dìdi gāo?_ (Is the older brother taller or the younger brother?).

**8. Question Words + "也/都" (yě/dōu) for "All" or "None"**

- **Structure description:** Because this specific structure relies on using question words (like "what" or "where") to make a _statement_ that means "everything" or "nothing," it does not have a direct question form in these materials.

**9. "跟" (gēn) Sentences for "With" or "From"**

- **Structure description:** To ask who someone is doing an activity with, replace the person in the statement with the question word "shéi" (who) directly after "gēn".
- **Example:** _Tā zài gēn shéi xué Xībānyáyǔ?_ (Who is she learning Spanish from?).
