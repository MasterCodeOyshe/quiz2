Here is an easy-to-understand overview of the different types of sentence structures taught in these materials:

**1. "把" (bǎ) Sentences for Handling Objects**

- **Structure description:** Subject + "bǎ" + Object + Verb + Other elements. This structure is used to indicate that an action has moved, changed, or resulted in something happening to a specific object.
- **Example:** _Qǐng bǎ wàitào tuō xiàlai._ (Please take off your coat.)

**2. "会" (huì) Sentences for Future Possibility**

- **Structure description:** Subject + "huì" + Verb. This is used to express that an action or event will probably happen in the future.
- **Example:** _Nǎge duì huì yíng?_ (Which team will win?)

**3. "了" (le) Sentences for Completed Actions**

- **Structure description:** Verb + "le" + Number + Measure Word + Object. This pattern shows that an action is fully completed, and it is usually followed by a specific quantity or description of the object.
- **Example:** _Wǒ kànle yì chǎng Zhōngwén diànyǐng._ (I saw a Chinese movie.)

**4. Double-Verb (Pivotal) Sentences**

- **Structure description:** Verb 1 (like "please", "ask", or "let") + Person + Verb 2. In this structure, the person receiving the first action becomes the one performing the second action.
- **Example:** _Qǐng xiàozhǎng fā jiǎng._ (Let's welcome the school principal to present the awards.)

**5. Sentences Showing Action Frequency**

- **Structure description:** Verb + Number + Action Measure Word (like "cì" for "times"). This is used to state exactly how many times an action was performed.
- **Example:** _Wǒ dǎle sān cì diànhuà._ (I called three times.)

**6. Sentences Showing Action Duration**

- **Structure description:** Verb + Time Duration + Object. By placing a period of time right after the verb, this sentence tells you how long an action has lasted. Adding "le" at the end shows the action is still ongoing.
- **Example:** _Wǒ xuéle liǎng nián Hànyǔ le._ (I've been learning Chinese for two years.)

**7. "比" (bǐ) Sentences for Comparisons**

- **Structure description:** A + "bǐ" + B + Adjective. This formula is used to compare two things, meaning "A is more [Adjective] than B." If you want to say A is _not as_ [Adjective] as B, you simply swap out "bǐ" for "méiyǒu".
- **Example:** _Zhège chéngshì bǐ nàge chéngshì nuǎnhuo._ (This city is warmer than that city.)

**8. Question Words + "也/都" (yě/dōu) for "All" or "None"**

- **Structure description:** When question words like "what", "who", or "where" are placed directly before "也" (also) or "都" (all), it changes their meaning to indicate "everything/everyone/everywhere". If used with a negative word, it means "nothing/no one/nowhere".
- **Example:** _Nà wǒ shénme yě méi dānwu._ (Then I've missed nothing.).

**9. "跟" (gēn) Sentences for "With" or "From"**

- **Structure description:** The word "跟" (gēn) acts as a preposition meaning "with" or "from". The structure is "跟" + Person + Verb, and it is used to show who you are doing an activity with, or who you are getting something from (like borrowing a book from a friend).
- **Example:** _Wǒ gēn lǎoshī xué Hànyǔ le._ (I studied Chinese with the help of my teacher.).
